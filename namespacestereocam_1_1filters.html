Drop pixels at depth discontinuities (edge / halo removal).

Over the valid 4-neighbours of each pixel \(z\) (only neighbours with depth \(> 0\) contribute), a pixel is zeroed when the largest one-sided depth jump exceeds a fraction rel_thresh of its own depth:

\[ \max\bigl(|\Delta_x z|,\,|\Delta_y z|\bigr) > \texttt{rel\_thresh}\cdot z \]

Single-pass, allocation-free, in-place safe (out may alias in).

Drop tiny speckle islands on a raw disparity map.

Mirrors cv::filterSpeckles: disparity is scaled by the fixed-point factor \(\times 16\) (4 fractional bits) into int16, connected components no larger than max_size whose neighbour difference stays within max_diff disparity units are zeroed. Zeroed pixels become 0.0f in out; survivors keep their original float value. Not in-place safe (out must differ from in).

Statistical outlier removal on an organized point cloud.

For each valid pixel \(i\) ( \(XYZ \neq 0\)) the mean Euclidean distance \(\bar d_i\) to its valid 8-grid neighbours is computed. Over the well-supported sample (pixels with at least min_neighbors valid neighbours) the mean \(\mu\) and standard deviation \(\sigma\) of \(\bar d_i\) are estimated, and a pixel is dropped (set to \(XYZ = 0\)) when it is under-supported or lies in the distance tail:

\[ \text{drop}_i = \bigl(c_i < \texttt{min\_neighbors}\bigr) \;\lor\; \bigl(\bar d_i > \mu + \texttt{std\_ratio}\cdot\sigma\bigr) \]

where \(c_i\) is the valid-neighbour count. Runs in three passes (per-pixel \(\bar d_i,\,c_i\) → sample \(\mu,\sigma\) and threshold → copy/zero). Caller provides scratch_mean (h*w float32) and scratch_cnt (h*w int32) so the kernel is allocation-free.

Per-pixel exponential moving average against the previous frame.

Where both the current pixel \(c\) and previous pixel \(p\) are valid ( \(> 0\)), the output is the blend

\[ o = \alpha\,c + (1-\alpha)\,p \]

otherwise \(c\) passes through. Lower \(\alpha\) ⇒ heavier weight on history ⇒ stronger temporal smoothing. prev is updated in place with the result wherever cur was valid. Caller supplies a shape-matching prev scratch buffer; the kernel is allocation-free.
