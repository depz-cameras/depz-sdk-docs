Detailed Description

Disparity-to-depth and point-cloud conversion utilities.

These free functions implement the geometric conversions used in the modular pipeline (stages 4 and 5). They operate on cv::Mat and have no dependency on the pipeline lifecycle.

Stereo depth formula

For a rectified, horizontally-aligned stereo pair the disparity \(d = u_L - u_R\) (the horizontal pixel shift of a point between the left and right images) maps to metric depth by the inverse-proportional relation

\[ Z = \frac{f_x \, B}{d} \]

where \(f_x\) is the focal length in pixels, \(B\) is the stereo baseline in millimetres, and \(d\) is the disparity in pixels. The result \(Z\) is in millimetres. The relation is singular as \(d \to 0\) ( \(Z \to \infty\)), so non-positive / sub-threshold disparities are rejected rather than divided.

Pinhole back-projection

A depth pixel \((u, v, Z)\) unprojects to a camera-frame 3-D point through the inverse pinhole model

\[ X = \frac{(u - c_x)\, Z}{f_x}, \qquad Y = \frac{(v - c_y)\, Z}{f_y}, \qquad Z = Z \]

with principal point \((c_x, c_y)\) and focal lengths \((f_x, f_y)\) taken from the rectified left camera matrix.

Q-matrix reprojection

Equivalently, OpenCV's 4×4 reprojection matrix \(Q\) maps a disparity pixel directly to homogeneous 3-D coordinates

\[ \begin{bmatrix} X \\ Y \\ Z \\ W \end{bmatrix} = Q \begin{bmatrix} u \\ v \\ d \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \qquad \mathbf{p} = \left(\frac{X}{W},\; \frac{Y}{W},\; \frac{Z}{W}\right) \]

after the homogeneous divide by \(W\). The point is expressed in the same units as \(B\) (millimetres).
