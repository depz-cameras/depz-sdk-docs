Detailed Description

Stereo Kannala–Brandt fisheye calibration with lon/lat (ERP) rectification.

Rectifies both eyes onto a shared equirectangular canvas and triangulates the azimuth disparity into a slant range; mono fisheye is unsupported because the lon/lat geometry needs the stereo baseline.

◆ disparity_to_depth()

cv::Mat stereocam::calibration::OpenCVFisheyeCalibration::disparity_to_depth ( const cv::Mat &  disparity_canvas_px) const

Per-pixel spherical-stereo triangulation of ERP disparity to slant range.

A canvas disparity of \(d\) pixels is an azimuth offset \(\Delta\theta = d\,(\text{FoV}_h / W)\) between the left ray \(\theta_L\) and the right ray \(\theta_R = \theta_L - \Delta\theta\). The law of sines on the camera–camera–point triangle gives the slant range

\[ r = \frac{B\,\cos\theta_R}{\cos\varphi\,\sin\Delta\theta}, \]

where \(B\) is the baseline and \(\varphi\) the elevation; the \(\cos\varphi\) term lifts the planar result onto the latitude.

Returns
Slant range in metres, H×W CV_32F (0 where disparity is invalid).
