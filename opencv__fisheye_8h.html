Detailed Description

Kannala–Brandt fisheye Calibration with lon/lat (ERP) rectified depth.

OpenCVFisheyeCalibration models the equidistant fisheye lens, where a ray at incidence angle \(\theta=\operatorname{atan}(r)\) (with \(r\) the normalized image radius) projects through the distortion polynomial

\[ \theta_d = \theta\,(1 + k_1\theta^2 + k_2\theta^4 + k_3\theta^6 + k_4\theta^8). \]

Rather than rectifying onto a virtual pinhole frame (which degenerates past \(\pm 90^\circ\) for wide-FoV lenses), it rectifies onto a longitude/latitude (equirectangular, ERP) canvas where every pixel covers an equal solid angle of (azimuth × elevation). Disparity is an azimuth difference, so depth is a slant range (Euclidean distance from the rectified-left origin), not z-forward depth — flattening to z-forward is meaningless at large azimuth.
